Understanding the float property

The float property is used to position elements to the left or right of their container. Elements with float: left; will align to the left, while those with float: right; will align to the right. Other content in the container will wrap around the floated elements.

Usage:

  • float: left;: Positions the element to the left of the container, allowing text or other content to wrap around it.
  • float: right;: Positions the element to the right of the container, allowing text or other content to wrap around it.

Clear Property:

To prevent content from wrapping around floated elements, we use the clear property. Setting clear: both; ensures that the content is placed below the floated elements and doesn't overlap them.
